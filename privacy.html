Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-18 · Last updated: 2026-07-18

Local-first · No uploads

CenturisTime is designed around a simple principle: your attendance data belongs to you and stays on your machine. This policy explains what information the application handles, where it is stored, and who (if anyone) it is shared with.

Data Collected

CenturisTime processes the following categories of data locally on your device:

Device connection credentials

The IP address, port, and password you enter to connect to an attendance device are stored only in the local application database on your computer. They are never transmitted to CenturisTime, its developers, or any third party.

Attendance records

Attendance logs, punch records, and timestamps pulled from your devices, as well as employee and user records, are stored locally in an encrypted SQLite database. This data is not uploaded anywhere.

License validation

To activate the software, you provide a Hardware ID derived from your machine and load a signed license file (.lic). The Hardware ID is a one-way fingerprint used solely to bind the license to your device. It is not sent to any server for validation — verification happens entirely on your machine.

Local preferences

UI settings such as your selected language (English or Arabic) and saved connection profiles are kept in local application storage.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal or attendance data with anyone. Because all data resides on your device and is never uploaded, there is no data to share.

Data Storage & Security

All stored data lives in an encrypted local SQLite database (attendance.db.enc) on your computer, protected with AES-256-GCM using a machine-bound key. The application communicates only with the attendance devices you explicitly configure, over your local network.

Third-Party Services

CenturisTime does not embed analytics, trackers, or advertising SDKs. The only outbound network activity is:

  • Automatic updates — the app checks a public update manifest and downloads a new installer when available. No personal data is sent with this request.
  • License requests — only if you choose to contact the developer (e.g., by opening a support issue) and voluntarily include your Hardware ID.

Data Retention & Deletion

Your data remains on your device for as long as you use the application. To remove everything, simply uninstall CenturisTime and delete its local data directory — no separate account or server-side data exists to purge.

Your Rights

Because no personal data leaves your machine, there is no server-side profile to access, correct, or delete. You remain in full control of your data at all times through the application and your operating system's file management.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as the product evolves. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, noted in release notes.

Contact

Questions about this policy? Open an issue in the CenturisTime releases repository.
